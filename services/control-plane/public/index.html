Capabilities

Everything a modern reviewer does, plus the lanes the incumbents can't reach

Cavix matches CodeRabbit and Greptile feature for feature, then separates on trust, cross-repo reach, and where it can be deployed.

The moat
1

Execution-grounded verification

Reproduces the bug (or runs a PoC exploit) in a sealed sandbox, applies the fix, re-runs your tests. Only proven findings are posted, so it never gets muted.

2

Cross-repo impact graph

Models your services, API contracts and shared libs. When a PR changes an interface, it flags the exact call sites that break in other repos, on every paid tier.

3

Whole-repo semantic graph

Symbols, call graph, data-flow and history give it the cross-file context to catch architectural bugs a diff-only tool can't see.

4

Multi-agent ensemble

Seven specialists, correctness, security, concurrency, performance, API/breaking, standards, tests, vote, then calibrate to your team's accept/reject bar.

5

CI/CD regression prediction

Ingests your build/test/perf history to warn about slow builds and p95 regressions before merge. A lane nobody else covers.

6

BYOK, model-agnostic, air-gapped

Run Claude, GPT, Gemini, or your own open model. Self-host with zero outbound calls and zero data retention, the enterprise wedge.

7

Learns & modernizes

An accept/reject learning loop tunes to your standards; legacy breadth (COBOL/PL-SQL/.NET) with verified migrations.

Verified fix PRs

Opens its own fix pull requests, but only fixes it proved green, always as a draft a human approves. Never auto-merges.
